# Salary Negotiation Tips

Best practices and strategies for negotiating your salary and compensation package.

### Research Market Rates
- Use [Levels.fyi](https://www.levels.fyi), Glassdoor, Payscale
- Consider location, company size, and your experience level
- Talk to recruiters and people in similar roles
- Factor in cost of living if relocating

### Know Your Minimum
- Determine your minimum acceptable salary
- Consider total compensation, not just base salary
- Factor in benefits, equity, bonuses
- Know when to walk away

### Prepare Your Value Proposition
- Quantify your achievements
- Reference your project cases from [../me/cases/](../me/cases/)
- Highlight unique skills or experiences
- Show how you'll contribute to their success

## ðŸ’¬ During Negotiation

### Timing
- **Wait for the offer** - Don't negotiate before you have an offer
- **Take time to consider** - It's okay to ask for 24-48 hours
- **Be the last to name a number** - If possible, let them make the first offer

### Communication
- **Be confident but respectful** - Show enthusiasm for the role
- **Use data** - Reference market research
- **Focus on value** - Emphasize what you bring to the table
- **Be flexible** - Consider alternative forms of compensation

### Phrases to Use
- "Based on my research and experience, I was expecting something in the range of..."
- "I'm very excited about this opportunity. Given my experience with [specific achievement], I was hoping we could discuss..."
- "I appreciate the offer. I was hoping we could explore a salary of [amount] based on [reason]..."
- "I understand the constraints. Could we discuss [alternative: equity, signing bonus, review timeline]?"

- **Accepting the first offer** - Most companies expect negotiation
- **Being too aggressive** - Can damage the relationship
- **Focusing only on salary** - Total compensation matters
- **Not getting it in writing** - Always get final offer in writing
- **Negotiating too early** - Wait until you have an offer

- **Stay professional** - Even if negotiations don't go your way

If salary is non-negotiable, consider:
- **Signing bonus** - One-time payment
- **Equity/Stock options** - Can be valuable long-term
- **Review timeline** - Earlier performance review with raise potential
- **Additional vacation days** - Work-life balance
- **Professional development budget** - Training, conferences
- **Remote work flexibility** - If applicable
- **Title/Level** - Can impact future earnings
